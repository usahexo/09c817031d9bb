Which coffee shops will make the list?

The coffee shops that most people think of when they hear the term “third wave coffee” are likely to be on the list. But what about the smaller, lesser-known places? They could easily make the list, too. In fact, there are many excellent coffee shops that don’t get as much attention as they deserve. So, which ones will make the cut?

Let’s take a look at some of the most popular third wave coffee shops and see which ones are worth visiting.

  1. Blue Bottle Coffee

Blue Bottle Coffee is one of the most well-known third wave coffee chains in the United States. It was founded in Oakland, California in 2002 and has since expanded to multiple states. Blue Bottle Coffee is known for its high-quality coffee beans and meticulous brewing methods.

  1. Starbuck’s

Starbuck’s may not be associated with third wave coffee as much as some of the other shops on this list, but it still deserves a mention. The company has been roasting and brewing its own coffee since 1971 and was one of the early adopters of the third wave movement. Starbuck’s now offers a wide range of specialty drinks and espresso-based beverages.

  1. The Wormhole Coffee

The Wormhole Coffee is a Chicago-based cafe that specializes in pour-over coffees. It was founded in 2013 by two brothers who were passionate about sourcing and roasting high-quality beans. The Wormhole Coffee is frequently praised for its delicious coffees and relaxed atmosphere.

  1. PT’s Coffee

PT’s Coffee is a Kansas City-based roaster that has been around since 1997. It started out as a small cafe but has since expanded to include several retail locations throughout Kansas City. PT’s is known for its intense focus on quality and for using a variety of brewing methods to extract flavor from each bean.

  1. Batdorf & Bronson
    Founded in Olympia, Washington in 1993, Batdorf & Bronson was one of the first companies to specialize in artisanal coffees. It has since grown into a national chain with locations throughout Washington, Oregon, California, and Arizona. Batdorf & Bronson is known for its high-quality beans, meticulous roasting processes, and unique brewing methods
